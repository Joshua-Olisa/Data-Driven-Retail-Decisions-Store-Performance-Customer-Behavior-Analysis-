A function to Annotate bars on the given Axes object with their height value.¶
In [23]:
def annotate_bars(ax, threshold=0.01):
    """
    Annotate bars on the given Axes object with their height value.
    
    Parameters:
    - ax: The Axes object containing the bar plot.
    - threshold: A value to filter out annotations for very small bars.
    """
    for p in ax.patches:
        height = p.get_height()
        
        # Only annotate if the height is greater than the threshold
        if height > threshold:
            ax.annotate(f'{height:.2f}', 
                        (p.get_x() + p.get_width() / 2., height), 
                        ha='center', va='center', 
                        fontsize=10, color='black', 
                        xytext=(0, 5), textcoords='offset points')

Overall Insights:¶

Older demographics, particularly Older Families, Older Singles/Couples, and Retirees, are the largest contributors to chip sales, showing a strong preference for larger pack sizes (110g, 150g, and 175g). These customers exhibit bulk-buying behavior, making them a key target for promotions and loyalty programs. Conversely, younger demographics, such as Young Singles/Couples and New Families, prefer smaller pack sizes (70g and 90g), indicating an opportunity to design products or promotions tailored to their needs.

Seasonal trends highlight peaks in sales during December, likely due to holiday festivities, and dips in February. Businesses can leverage this by increasing marketing efforts, running promotions, or offering value packs during peak periods to maximize revenue.

The consistent pricing across customer segments suggests a strategy that works well for mainstream and premium customers. However, there is potential to attract more budget-conscious segments by offering occasional discounts or smaller, affordable packs.

To grow chip sales further, focus should be placed on:

  • Targeting Older Customers: Use loyalty programs and promotions on larger pack sizes to retain and grow high-value segments.
  • Engaging Younger Demographics: Develop marketing strategies around convenience and affordability, such as smaller pack bundles or student discounts.
  • Seasonal Campaigns: Capitalize on seasonal peaks (e.g., December) with festive-themed promotions and offers.
  • Product Diversification: Maintain a balanced product mix to appeal to both bulk-buying and occasional-purchase behaviors across different life stages.
